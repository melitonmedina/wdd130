History

Firs a little history of white water rafting according to whitewaterrafting.com "[it] can be traced back to 1811 when the first recorded attempt to navigate the Snake River in Wyoming was planned. With no training, experience, or proper equipment, the river was found to be too difficult and dangerous. Hence, it was given the nickname “Mad River.”

Water Riders came about from a desired to share how exhilirating and breath taking the sport can be. So in 2008 the very first trip took place down the colorado river. Since then thousands of adventure seekers have joined one the several trips offered by us.
